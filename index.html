One score. Every bird. Before and during the flight.

Built for pilots flying Spain and Portugal, BirdRisk does one thing well: it tells you how risky birds make your route — on the ground when you plan it, and in the air as you fly it — clearly enough to glance at on a kneeboard.

Plan it on the ground

Draw a route and BirdRisk colours each segment by bird-strike risk — by hour of day and altitude band — from real migration, wetland and radar data.

Fly with it live

In the air, your aircraft tracks over the 3D terrain with the risk right now and just ahead — plus a quiet heads-up the moment you enter a busier area.

You stay in command

BirdRisk informs — it never tells you to go or no-go. The final call is always yours, the way aviation works.

In the air, at a glance

Risk now. Risk ahead.

In flight, BirdRisk follows your aircraft over the terrain and reads the bird-strike risk where you are — and where you're heading. A quiet banner flags the moment you cross into a higher-risk area. Kneeboard-clear, no menus.

Tap to understand

Tap a zone. Know why — and when.

Every risk area is explainable. Tap it to see the level, the coordinates and the dominant factor behind it — then open the hourly trend to find the calmer window. Birds move at dawn and dusk; BirdRisk shows you when.
